Space Rover Docking System

Developed and modeled tensegrity-based docking mechanisms for space-docking rover systems, optimizing structural efficiency and minimizing weight. Implemented 3-degree-of-freedom actuation system with MATLAB simulations and Arduino integration for motion control and deployment precision.

Static Double Helix Tensegrity Models

As an undergraduate researcher, I designed, modeled, and fabricated multiple static double-helix tensegrity prototypes to validate structural stability and inform the development of the dynamic version. Using simulation-driven analysis and experimental testing, I optimized the geometry and material configuration to enhance load distribution and demonstrate the feasibility of deployable tensegrity structures for space applications.

MATLAB SolidWorks Python 3D Printing

Dynamic Double Helix Tensegrity Model

As an undergraduate researcher, I designed, simulated, and built a dynamic double-helix tensegrity model capable of actuation and compression at the press of a button. The project served as a proof of concept for optimizing deployable space structures, integrating structural simulations, control systems, and rapid prototyping to demonstrate adaptive and efficient tensegrity behavior.

MATLAB SolidWorks Arduino
